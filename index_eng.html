TOP 10

MOST BEAUTIFUL LANSCAPES OF HÉRAULT DC

Sea, rivers, garrigues, mountains, typical and historic villages trace our history. Here are some paths to tread at the heart of our unmissable landscapes.

FOLLOW THE GUIDE!

10

The pond of Montady

Recognizable among a thousand, these sun-drenched cultures stem from the draining of an ancient marsh. It is from the hill of Ensérune that one truly appreciates this hydro-agricultural masterpiece conceived during the Middle Ages

9

Volcanic cliffs of Cap d’Agde

La Grande Conque, an authentic volcanic beach, welcomes Les Rochers des 2 Frères in one of its coves. These black rocks are typical of Agde. From the promontory overlooking the Mediterranean, one can spend hours observing these two brothers located at the edge of the cliff and enjoying a viewpoint of Cap d’Agde.

8

The Haut-Languedoc

Often rugged, here are the reliefs of the Parc Naturel Régional du Haut-Languedoc. When coming from the plains, one faces this succession of massifs. Then, we learn to recognize them. And quickly comes the desire to know each peak. From their summits, the gaze stretches all the way to the sea. Some winding roads climb through this rocky fortress. They weave through the heart of deciduous and coniferous forests, and finally reach the shores of lakes—true blue islands amidst the green.

7

The Lagoon of Thau

Calm, serene, adorned with a beautiful blue that reflects the sun’s rays throughout the day, the Thau Basin does not leave anyone indifferent. No matter where you are, Sète is in your line of sight. Oyster farmers tend to their shellfish beds. Taste! From producer to consumer, no intermediaries—truly a delightful freshness.

6

The vineyards of Saint-Chinian

From the Fontjun Pass or the Orb Valley, a landscape of vineyards unfolds. Wine is the order of the day here! It has its home in the center of the village of St-Chinian. Beyond, far from the vineyards, a shape emerges… that of a reclining woman, Mont Caroux. Yet another Hérault legend to discover!

5

The Hérault valley

Enter the Hérault Gorges through the Pont du Diable beach. An astonishing rocky landscape awaits you there. Steep and winding gorges where the water meanders peacefully... Whether swimming or canoeing, appreciate this typology. Explore a narrow and rugged valley with diverse flora and fauna. And how can you not take advantage of stopping at Saint-Guilhem-Le-Désert?

4

The Cathedral of Maguelone and its Lagoons

Wherever you are, the Cathedral of Maguelone perched on its peninsula will be your beacon! Surrounded by ponds and the Mediterranean Sea, only a small piece of land connects it to the coast. A privileged location! Beyond the ponds, you can distinguish Palavas and the salt pans of Maguelone. Observe the pink flamingos and egrets, masters of the place…

3

From Petit au Grand Travers, a long beach of fine sand

Walking barefoot on our fine sandy beaches provides a true sense of well-being. From Carnon, scan the horizon… your gaze will be drawn to La Grande-Motte… Not a single dwelling until this seaside resort. The unique buildings created by architect Jean Baladur gradually emerge like a mirage. Pure pleasure!

2

The Cirque de Navacelles

From the heights of Baume Auriol, behold the oyster of Larzac. This land promontory emerges from the meanders of the Vis River. An astonishing geological feature. Who would imagine that one of Europe’s largest canyons lies here? Water flows through this limestone and arid landscape of Larzac! Embark on a hike to unravel the mystery…

1

The Salagou lake

The “ruffe,” warm and welcoming red earth, encircles this lake with a thousand facets. A wild space reminiscent of the Far West. Nothing is flat here—perfect for walking, mountain biking, or horseback riding. Quaint villages from another era line the shores of Lake Salagou. How incredible to think that this lake is artificial!

How can you come visit us and not be surprised throughout your stay by the diversity of Hérault ?
